Rebate #1

Solar Victoria Hot Water Rebate

A direct rebate from the Victorian Government for households upgrading to a heat pump hot water system. Means-tested but generous when eligible.

How much?

$1,000 standard rebate — or $1,400 for Australian-made systems like the Reclaim and Rheem ranges.

Eligibility

  • Combined household income less than $210,000/year
  • Property value less than $3 million
  • Existing hot water system being replaced is more than 3 years old
  • Haven't previously claimed the Solar Victoria battery rebate
Rebate #2

Small-scale Technology Certificates (STCs)

When you install an eligible renewable energy system, you earn a number of STCs — certificates with a tradeable cash value that helps reduce the upfront cost of going renewable.

How much?

Approximately $600–$650 per heat pump install — value depends on your zoning.

Eligibility

  • All heat pump installs are eligible — including new homes and rental properties
  • System must meet Australian renewable energy standards
  • Installer must be accredited (we are)

STCs support the transition to cleaner energy by making renewable systems more affordable, improving grid stability, and helping lower electricity prices for everyone.

Rebate #3

Victorian Energy Efficiency Certificates (VEECs)

VEECs are digital credits generated through the Victorian Energy Upgrades program. Every certificate represents a 1-tonne reduction in CO₂-equivalent emissions.

How much?

$380–$550 — value depends on the efficiency rating of your chosen tank.

Eligibility

  • Existing gas or electric service connected to a gas/electric supply before decommissioning
  • Existing tank must still be on site and installed at quote time
  • Old heat pumps are not eligible (only gas/electric replacements)
